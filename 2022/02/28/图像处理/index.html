发表于 2022-02-28 更新于 2022-05-27 分类于 课程 阅读次数： Valine：

Digital Image Procession

Image Acquisition

An image is a projection of a 3D scene into a 2D
projection plane.

An image can be defined as a 2 variable function

$f(x,y): R^2→R$

Notations

• Image Intensity
– Light energy emitted from a unit area in the image
– Device dependence
**• Image Brightness **
– The subjective appearance of a unit area in the image
– Context dependence
– Subjective
• Image Gray-Level
– The relative intensity at each unit area
– Between the lowest intensity (Black value) and the highest
intensity (White value)
– Device independent

Weber Law

$Brightness ∝ log(f)$

Digitization

Spatial sampling: Spatial domain

Quantization: Gray level

The density of the sampling denotes the separation capability of the resulting image

Image resolution defines the finest details that are still visible by the image

Nyquist Frequency

Nyquist Rule: To observe details at frequency f (wavelength d) one must sample at frequency > 2f (sampling intervals < d/2)

The Frequency 2f is the Nyquist Frequency

Non-uniform Quantization

Quantize according to visual sensitivity (Weber’s Law)

Optimal Quantization (Lloyd -Max)

$Llogyds: $

a. 首先随机选择 个站点
b. 对其他点进行划分，形成 单元
c. 计算每个单元的质心，更新相应的站点
d. 迭代b、c这两个步骤，直至收敛

Image Histogram

Image Characteristics

Image Contrast

Global Contrast

$max{ I ( x, y)} − min{ I ( x, y)}$

$var{ I ( x, y)} = mean{(I ( x, y) - I_{av})^2}$

Local Image Contrast

$$
C = \mid \frac {I_p - I_n} {I_n} \mid
$$

$I_{NEW}(x,y)= α·I(x,y)+ β$

Image Statistics

$N = \sum_kH(k)$

$P(k) = H(k)/N$

$C(k) = \sum^k H(i)$
$$
E(I) = \frac 1 N \sum_k kH(k) = \sum_kkP(k)
$$

Image Entropy

$$
Entropy(I)=-\sum_kP(k)logP(k)
$$

Histogram Usage

KL Distance

$$
D_{KL}(A||B) = \sum_kP_A(k)log(\frac{P_A(k)}{P_B(k)})
$$

==Measures the amount of added information needed to encode image A based on the histogram of image B.==

Suffers from the same drawback of the Minkowski distance.

The Earth Mover Distance (EMD)

$$
D_{EMD}(A,B) = min_F(\sum_i \sum_jf_{ij}* d_{ij}) \
s.t. \ f_{ij} \ge 0;P_B(k)=\sum_if_{ik});P_A(k)=\sum_if_{ki});
$$

没理解错的话就是最小费用最大流的二分图版本

Point Operations

$$
g(x,y) = \alpha f(x,y) + \beta
$$

$$
M ( v ) = αv + β\
\alpha = \frac{q’ - p’}{q - p};\beta = p’ - \alpha p
$$

Histogram Equalization

Our goal: finding a gray-level transformation $M(v)$
such that:
– The histogram $H_b$ is as flat as possible.
– The order of gray-levels is maintained.
– The histogram bars are not fragmented.

更平，更宽，更连续

Histogram matching

• Transforms an image A so that its histogram will match that of another image B.

• Usage: before comparing two images of the same scene (change detection) acquired under different lighting conditions or different camera parameters.

Geometric Transformation

一些集合变化

Forward Mapping

Problems with forward mapping due to sampling:

– Holes (some target pixels are not populated)

– Overlaps (some target pixels assigned few colors)

Inverse Mapping

• Each target pixel assigned a single color.
• Color Interpolation is required.

Interpolation

Interpolation: generates a new pixel by analyzing the surrounding pixels.

Bilinear Interpolation

Spatial Filtering

Convolution

$$
I’(u,v) = \sum_{(i,j)\in R_H} I(u-i,v-j)\cdot H(i,j) \
I’ = I * H
$$

Linear Operators

$$
F(cI) = cF(I) \
F(I_1 + I_2) = F(I_1) + F(I_2)
$$

Properties of Convolution

$$
IH = HI
$$

$$
(I ∗ H1) ∗ H2 = I ∗ (H1 ∗ H2)
$$

$$
(a · I) ∗ H = a · (I ∗ H) \
(I1 + I2) ∗ H = (I1 ∗ H) + (I2 ∗ H)
$$

$$
S(I ∗ H) = S(I) ∗ H
$$

The only shift-invariant, linear operators on images are convolutions

Gaussian

1D
$$
g_{\sigma}(x) = \frac 1 {\sqrt{2 \pi} \sigma}exp(-\frac{x^2}{2\sigma ^ 2})
$$
2D
$$
G_{\sigma}(x,y) = \frac 1 {2 \pi \sigma ^ 2}exp(-\frac{x^2 + y^2}{2\sigma ^ 2})
$$

$$
G(x,y) = g(x) \cdot g(y)
$$
